# First-Time Parent Walkthrough — MoonBell

**Persona:** Priya, 33, Bengaluru. Found MoonBell via an Instagram reel while looking for a **5th-birthday gift** for her son Aarav. Mid-range Android (Redmi), 4G, one hand, baby on the other hip. Has never heard of the brand. Protective of Aarav's photos and data. Pays by **UPI**. Will bounce the second something feels complicated, unfinished, or asks too much too soon.

This is a moment-by-moment record of what she thinks, expects, and where she'd leave. It maps to the live site + funnel as built today.

---

## 0. The tap from Instagram
- **Sees:** a reel of an illustrated child hero. Taps "link in bio."
- **Expects:** to instantly see a book with a kid in it, a price, and a way to try it.
- **Reality:** lands on the homepage. First words in the content area: **"PRIVATE ALPHA FOR FIRST FAMILIES."**
- **Thought:** *"Alpha? Is this even open? Is it a real company or someone's side project?"* First flicker of doubt before she's read the headline.

## 1. Above the fold
- **Sees:** "Make your child the hero of their own storybook," a warm illustration of a mum and child reading, "Create your free preview," and "Built for known-family testing."
- **Thinks:** *"Okay, cute. But that's a picture of some other kid reading — where's the actual book I'd get? What does MINE look like?"*
- **Expects after tapping "Create your free preview":** to start describing Aarav and see something.
- **Friction:** "Built for known-family testing" makes her wonder if she's allowed in. The headline is a *claim*; nothing on screen *shows* personalisation. **She almost puts the phone down here — but the word "free" keeps her.**

## 2. She taps "Create your free preview"
- **Expects:** a form — nickname, age, done.
- **Reality:** the page bounces to **Sign in** ("Let's make their book — we'll email you a magic link" / Continue with Google).
- **Thought:** *"Wait, I have to make an account before I've even seen anything? I don't know you yet."*
- **This is the first hard abandonment point.** A cautious gift-browser will not create an account on faith. If she pushes on with Google (because it's one tap), she continues; most won't.

## 3. (If she signs in) The beta wall
- **Reality:** after signing in she hits **"Private beta — enter your beta code."**
- **Thought:** *"A code? I don't have a code. So I actually can't use this."*
- **Second hard abandonment point, and a worse one** — she's now given an email/Google account and gotten *nothing*, then been told she's not allowed. That's the profile of someone who never comes back and tells a friend "it doesn't really work yet."

> For 100% of real Instagram traffic, the journey effectively **ends by step 3** today. Everything below is what *should* happen — and what a redesigned funnel must deliver.

---

## What the journey *should* feel like (target)

## 1′. Above the fold (redesigned)
- **Sees:** a book cover that already says **"Aarav and the Star That Listens"**, and a little field: *"Type your child's name."*
- **She types "Aarav."** The cover title updates to his name. A gentle page-opening animation plays once.
- **Thought:** *"Oh! That's his name on a real book cover. Okay, I want to see this."*
- **Value arrives in ~4 seconds, with no account.** This is the moment the reel promised.

## 2′. Proof: a real sample book
- **Sees:** one finished cover + two readable spreads she can swipe, with actual story text and the same hero on every page. Caption: "16–20 pages · PDF · delivered in ~2 minutes."
- **Thought:** *"The art's consistent and the writing's sweet — not creepy AI. This is giftable."*
- **Question answered:** "Is it actually good?" — before being asked for anything.

## 3′. "Create their free preview" (now she's warm)
- **Enters:** nickname (Aarav), age (4–6), a couple of appearance picks, interests (dinosaurs, space), personality (curious, brave), and a **goal** ("bedtime courage" for the birthday theme).
- **Reassurance right under the form:** *"No photos. A nickname, not a real name. Delete anytime."*
- **Thought:** *"They're not asking for a photo or his real name — good. This feels safe."* Her core fear is pre-empted at the exact moment it spikes.

## 4′. The reveal (emotional peak)
- **Sees:** a short, charming generation animation ("drawing Aarav… setting the scene…"), then **his personalised cover + the opening spread**.
- **Thought / feeling:** *"That's him. That's actually him as the hero."* This is the moment she'd screenshot and send to her husband. **Peak emotion = maximum willingness to pay.**

## 5′. Now — and only now — the ask
- **Sees:** "Love it? **Unlock the complete story — ₹299.** Full 16–20 page illustrated book, personalised cover, one free revision, instant PDF. Pay by UPI or card."
- **Then:** a light email/account step to save it, and **UPI checkout**.
- **Thought:** *"₹299 for something this personal? That's less than the toy I was going to buy — and he'll keep this."*

## 6′. Confirmation & delivery
- **Sees:** "Your book is being finished — it'll be in your account in ~2 minutes. We'll email the download." Clear status, clear next step.
- **Thought:** *"Done. That was easy and it's genuinely special."*

## The three sentences that summarise the problem
1. We ask a stranger for an account and a code **before** we show her anything.
2. We **describe** the magic ("make your child the hero") instead of **doing** it on the page.
3. We tell her, repeatedly, that the product isn't really for sale yet.

Fix those three and the same Priya who bounces today converts — because the product itself is genuinely giftable; the funnel is what's hiding it.
